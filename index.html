Scottish Norwegian Society (Glasgow)

Welcome – Velkommen

Welcome

Welcome to the Scottish Norwegian Society website. You can also find us on Facebook.

We are a group of people in and around Glasgow and further afield with an interest in Norway. We meet monthly at various social and cultural events. From October to May we hold our meetings in the Kelvinside Hillhead Parish Church’s meeting rooms in the west end of Glasgow, about five minutes’ walk from Byres Road and Hillhead Underground Station.

Our Meetings

Meetings usually begin around 7:30 PM, with the formal part starting 15–20 minutes later, giving time for a chat and a chance to keep your Norwegian or English language skills up.

The new season begins in September with a social gathering or film night. In October we have the first of our planned syllabus talks. November normally includes two events: another syllabus talk and our popular Christmas Fair (Julebasar), where members contribute home arts and crafts.

In December, when numbers allow, we arrange a Christmas Tree Party (Juletrefest) for the children after the Church’s Norwegian Service, often concluding with a St Lucia procession.

Events Through the Year

With the new year, we enjoy a musical evening where members sing and play instruments. February brings our annual lecture, originating from a legacy by Oddveig Røsegg, who taught Norwegian at Glasgow University.

March features a syllabus event, and April is our “ventures forth” outing to a place of Norwegian interest. Past visits include the Govan Stones, Stirling Castle, the Viking Hoard at the National Museum of Scotland, and Glengoyne Distillery.

May is a major month for Norwegians, with celebrations for 17th May – Norway’s National Day.

The final event of our season is Sankthans (midsummer), usually a BBQ near the 21st of June, often held at Mugdock Park near Milngavie.

Stay Connected

To stay up to date with our events, visit our Facebook page or check this website regularly.

A Bit of History

The Society’s beginnings go back to 1941, when Norwegian exiles and refugees were hosted in and around Dumfries. Many trained as soldiers and saboteurs during the Second World War.

The Scottish Norwegian Society (Glasgow), formed in 1943, is the only surviving branch of the original Society founded in Dumfries. In 2016 we marked the 75th anniversary with a special dinner and a commemorative service at St Michael’s Church, Dumfries.
